Hi, I'm Evan Batteas. I graduated from Texas A&M University in May 2025 with a BS in Physics and minors in Mathematics and Astrophysics. My main research interest is in Cosmology, specifically in constraining the properties of Dark Matter.

My Work


Astrophysics:

I've done all sorts of work in Astrophysics. I started off working on type 1a supernovae, then moved to instrumention with designing a spectrograph, then into gamma-ray analysis of dark matter halos using Fermipy, which is now my main project. I also worked on constraining the mass of the local group in a warm dark matter universe by using the timing argument on Milky Way and Andromeda analogues in simulations as part of the fifth iteration of the EXPLORE program, upgrading a self-interacting dark matter simulation to include dissipation as part of the GREP program, and observing two RR Lyrae, XZ Cyg and DE Lac as part of the ChETEC-INFRA Observational School.

Teaching:

I've been a Supplemental Instruction Leader at TAMU, and a tutor and Extending the Class Leader at Blinn College.

Outreach:

I've participated in outreach both at my home university, Texas A&M, and during the GREP program in Frankfurt, Germany.
