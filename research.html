Constraining Dark Matter Annihilation in dSphs

Advisor: Prof. Louis Strigari  | Texas A&M University

I've recently started a new project under Prof. Strigari, where I will be using the updated 16 years of Fermi-LAT data to do a combined analysis of dSphs. I will also use J-factors calculated for velocity-dependent annihilation to analyze the velocity-dependent annihilation in these dSphs.

NOT Observations of XZ Cyg and DE Lac

Advisor: Prof. Camilla Juul Hansen  | CHINOS

In July of 2025, I attended the 2nd iteration of the ChETEC-INFRA Observational School, held in Prague. The school was held at Villa Lanna in Prague and organized by Dr. Brankica Kubátová. There, I listened to lectures about nuclear physics, observational astrophysics, and science in general. We also went on a guided tour of the Ondřejov Observatory, and an “astronomical tour” of Prague. During the school, we had the opportunity to perform remote observing using the Nordic Optical Telescope (NOT). We split up into teams, and my team was headed by Prof. Camilla Juul Hansen. Our goal was to observe two RR Lyrae stars, XZ Cyg and DE Lac. We were able to get 4 observations of XZ Cyg and 3 observations of DE Lac at different points in their period. After observing these stars, we worked on analyzing their spectra using WebSME, looking for the radial velocity, temperature, and other properties and abundances of the stars. After the observational school was over, we continued working together as a team, and Dr. Zdenek Prudil joined our team and provided us with software, which we are currently using to continue our analysis of the stars.

Constraining Dark Matter Annihilation in the LMC

Advisor: Prof. Louis Strigari  | Texas A&M University

I analyzed gamma-ray emission from the Large Magellanic Cloud using Fermi-LAT data to place constraints on dark matter annihilation cross sections. This involved spatial modeling, J-factor construction, and spectral fitting.
